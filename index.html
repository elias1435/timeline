1086

The village appears in the Doomsday Book, it was then known as Phingaria or (Fingrith)

1152-1162

The Augustinian priory was founded

1213

The first mention of Blackmore was ‘La Blakemore’, in 1213, which Reaney (1935) translates as ‘black swamp’ referring to the low lying site amidst many springs

1232

The Prior was granted a charter to hold a three-day fayre at Blackmore on the Feast of St Laurence

1349

The plague or Black Death came to Essex in Spring 1349 by Autumn of that year 70 of Blackmore’s 85 families were wiped out. Redrose Lane was built to keep the plague away from the village

1385

The Bull inn was built, complete with priest hole and escape tunnel

1400

The Oak bell tower was added to the church of St Laurence then part of the priory

1519

Henry Fitzroy the illegitimate son of Henry V111 was born to Elizabeth Blount, Henry’s mistress Little Jericho, a house within the grounds of the priory in Blackmore

1525

Henry Fitzroy at 6 years old was elevated to knight of the Garter and made Earl of Noingham, and then Duke of both Richmond and somerset

1527

The priory at Blackmore was dissolved by Cardinal Wolsey under the instruction of Henry V111 Much of the Priory was demolished but part of the church was retained

1536

Henry Fitzroy died on 23 July at from consumption St James’s Palace. He was 17
